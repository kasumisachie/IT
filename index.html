What Is Sushi? Sushi is a Japanese dish that features medium-grained rice cooked in vinegar, served with raw or cooked seafood and a variety of toppings or fillings. Contrary to popular belief, rice is the main component of sushi, not raw fish.

PHO

Vietnamese cuisine is characterized by its balance of five elements: spicy, sour, bitter, salty, and sweet. The menu often includes dishes made from rice, noodles, fresh herbs, fruits, and vegetables. Seafood and pork are the most commonly used proteins, with dishes like pho, banh mi, and goi cuon being popular.
